efficiency tracker app idea — rough notes
==========================================

ok so the basic thing is: at the start of every task someone on the team
estimates how long it'll take. then when they finish they log the actual
time. we want to see the gap between estimated and actual, per person,
per week, per project.

why: half the team consistently under-estimates and the other half pads
estimates so much that we can't plan anything. nobody is doing this
maliciously, they just don't have feedback on their own track record.
giving them a dashboard with their own numbers should fix it for most
people without management having to nag.

who uses it
- everyone on the team logs their own tasks (devs, designers, pm, copy)
- team leads / managers see rollups for their direct reports
- one or two senior people / founders see everything across the company

basic flow per task:
1. user creates a task — title, project, estimate in hours (can be 0.25
   minimum)
2. they work on it, can pause/resume a timer if they want, or just
   manually enter actual hours when done
3. they mark it done — actual hours required field
4. system calculates variance = actual - estimated and a ratio
5. dashboard updates

dashboards
- personal dashboard: my last 30 days, my average variance, am I trending
  better or worse, weekly chart
- manager dashboard: each direct report as a row with their numbers,
  click into anyone to see their personal view
- top-level: company-wide variance, broken down by project and by team

weekly summary email goes out friday afternoon:
  - your variance this week vs last week
  - your worst-estimated task and your best-estimated task
  - 1 tip if your variance is bad (like "estimates over 8h are usually
    wrong, break them down")

things to be careful about
- people might forget to log. need a gentle nudge, not a guilt trip.
  maybe slack reminder at 4pm if nothing logged today.
- estimates change as the task is in progress. should we let people
  update the estimate? probably yes but track the original separately
  so the variance is honest.
- vacation / sick days shouldn't count against you. need an "out of
  office" toggle.
- tasks that span multiple days — log hours per day or just total?
  i think per day, otherwise the timer ux is weird.
- privacy: people get nervous when their time is tracked. only their
  direct manager + themselves see their data. nobody else by default.

look and feel
- green and white. clean. minimal. think notion or linear, not jira.
- definitely NOT corporate / enterprise feeling. this is a tool people
  should want to open.
- numbers and charts are the main content so typography needs to be
  great for tabular data. probably inter for everything plus a mono
  font for numbers.
- desktop / laptop first. people use this at their desk. mobile is
  nice-to-have, like quick "log my hours" but not the full dashboards.

things we're NOT building yet
- billing / invoicing / client time tracking — this is internal only
- integrations with jira/asana/linear (later, maybe)
- approval workflows (manager approves timesheet) — too corporate
- payroll integration — no
- mobile native app — pwa is enough

stuff i'm still not sure about
- should the timer auto-pause when you're idle? feels creepy
- do we let people see each other's variance or only their own +
  manager's view? leaning private but team might want a friendly
  leaderboard option
- weekly summary on friday afternoon or monday morning? probably friday
  so people can think about it over the weekend, but maybe monday
  morning catches people fresh
- 4-hour or 8-hour cap on a single task entry?

tech bits (i think)
- web app, react probably, ssr not required
- postgres for the data
- background job for the friday email
- need decent charts — maybe recharts or visx
- auth — probably just google sso since everyone has a work google

key data we're storing
- users (name, email, role, manager_id, team_id)
- tasks (title, project, estimate_hours, actual_hours, status, owner,
  started_at, completed_at, original_estimate)
- time_entries (task_id, user_id, hours, date, note) — for per-day logging
- projects (name, owner, archived)
- teams (name, lead_user_id)
- weekly_summaries (user_id, week_starting, variance, sent_at) — so we
  don't double-send

pages off the top of my head
- landing / marketing page (probably just internal so minimal)
- login (google sso)
- my dashboard (default after login)
- log a task / quick add
- task list (filter by project, status, date)
- task detail / edit
- team dashboard (managers only)
- company dashboard (founders only)
- settings (notification prefs, OOO toggle, timezone)
- profile (name, photo, role)
